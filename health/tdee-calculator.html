Formula

BMR (Mifflin-St Jeor): Male: 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age + 5 Female: 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age − 161 TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier Sedentary ×1.2, Light ×1.375, Moderate ×1.55, Active ×1.725, Very Active ×1.9

Example

A 30 year old male, 80 kg, 180 cm with moderate activity: BMR = 1780, TDEE = 1780 × 1.55 = 2759 kcal/day.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is TDEE? ▾
TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories you burn in a day, including your BMR plus calories burned through activity and exercise.
How do I use TDEE to lose or gain weight? ▾
Eating below your TDEE creates a calorie deficit for weight loss, while eating above it creates a surplus for weight gain.
