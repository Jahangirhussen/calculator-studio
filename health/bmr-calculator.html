Formula

Mifflin-St Jeor: Male: BMR = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age + 5 Female: BMR = 10×kg + 6.25×cm − 5×age − 161

Example

A 30 year old male, 80 kg, 180 cm: BMR = 10×80 + 6.25×180 − 5×30 + 5 = 1780 kcal/day.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is BMR? ▾
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body needs to maintain basic life functions while at complete rest.
Why does the Mifflin-St Jeor formula differ by gender? ▾
Men typically have more lean muscle mass than women, so the formula uses different constants to better estimate resting energy needs.
