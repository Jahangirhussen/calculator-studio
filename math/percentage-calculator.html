Formula

X% of Y = (X/100) × Y X is what % of Y = (X/Y) × 100 % change = ((Y − X)/X) × 100

Example

20% of 150 = 30. 30 is 20% of 150. Change from 100 to 120 is a 20% increase.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate percentage increase? ▾
Subtract the old value from the new value, divide by the old value, then multiply by 100.
Can the result be negative? ▾
Yes, a negative result for percentage change indicates a decrease.
