Quadratic Equation Solver

Solve ax² + bx + c = 0 for x, with real or complex roots.

Result
—

Formula

x = (−b ± √(b² − 4ac)) / 2a

Example

For x² − 5x + 6 = 0: x = 2 or x = 3.

How To Use

Enter coefficients a, b and c (a ≠ 0), then click Calculate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if a = 0? ▾
If a = 0 the equation is not quadratic; the calculator will show an error.
What are complex roots? ▾
When the discriminant (b² − 4ac) is negative, the roots are complex numbers shown in the form p ± qi.
