Advanced Work Shift Calculator

Add unlimited work phases — for example a split shift with a morning session and an evening session — and get the total hours worked. Handles overnight shifts crossing midnight automatically.

How It Works

Each phase is one continuous block of work — e.g. Phase 1: 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM, Phase 2: 2:00 PM to 6:00 PM. The calculator finds the duration of each phase, correctly handling overnight phases (e.g. 11:00 PM to 3:00 AM = 4 hours), and adds them all up for the total working time.

Example

Phase 1: 12:00 AM → 03:00 PM (15h). Phase 2: 04:00 PM → 07:30 PM (3h 30m). Total Working Time = 18h 30m.

Overnight example: Phase 1: 10:00 PM → 06:00 AM (8h, crosses midnight automatically).

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it handle overnight shifts? ▾
Yes. If the end time is earlier than the start time, the calculator assumes the shift continues into the next day.
Can I add unlimited phases? ▾
Yes, use "+ Add Phase" to add as many as needed, and delete or duplicate any phase.
What does 12 AM / 12 PM mean here? ▾
12 AM is midnight (00:00) and 12 PM is noon (12:00) — handled correctly per standard convention.
