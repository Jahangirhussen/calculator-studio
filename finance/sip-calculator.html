Estimate the maturity value of your monthly SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) investment.

Formula

FV = P × (((1+r)n − 1) / r) × (1+r)
r = monthly rate = annual rate / 12 / 100, n = number of months, P = monthly investment

Example

Investing $5,000 monthly at 12% annual return for 10 years (120 months): r = 0.01, maturity value ≈ $1,161,695 with total invested $600,000, giving a gain of ≈ $561,695.

How To Use

Enter your monthly investment amount, expected annual return rate, and duration in years, then click Calculate to see your estimated maturity value, total invested and estimated returns.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is SIP? ▾
A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount regularly (usually monthly) into mutual funds or other instruments, benefiting from rupee-cost averaging and compounding.
Are SIP returns guaranteed? ▾
No, SIP returns depend on market performance. This calculator uses an assumed constant annual return rate for estimation purposes only.
