Calculate how your money grows with compound interest at different compounding frequencies.

Formula

A = P × (1 + r/n)n×t
Compound Interest = A − P
r = annual rate/100, n = compounding periods per year, t = years

Example

Principal $10,000 at 6% for 5 years compounded quarterly: A = 10000×(1+0.06/4)^(4×5) ≈ $13,468.55. Compound interest ≈ $3,468.55.

How To Use

Enter the principal, annual interest rate, time in years, and select a compounding frequency, then click Calculate to see the final amount and interest earned.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is compound interest? ▾
Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods, causing money to grow faster than simple interest.
Does compounding frequency matter? ▾
Yes — more frequent compounding (e.g. monthly or daily) yields slightly more interest than annual compounding at the same nominal rate.
